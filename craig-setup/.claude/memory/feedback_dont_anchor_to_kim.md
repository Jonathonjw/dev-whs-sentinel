Don't bring up Kim or Embark Building when discussing Sentinal architecture, planning, features, positioning, or strategy unless the user explicitly mentions him in the current message.

**Why:** Kim is one customer, not the strategy. Anchoring platform/architecture decisions to "what Kim needs" misframes the work. The founding-customer context exists for background, not for citing in every reply.

**How to apply:**
- Keep platform/architecture conversations platform-grade. Justify scope/decisions on technical and product merits, not customer fit.
- Avoid phrases like "for Kim's builders", "Kim's sites", "good for Kim", "Kim will need".
- The only exception: when the user brings up Kim by name in the current message.
